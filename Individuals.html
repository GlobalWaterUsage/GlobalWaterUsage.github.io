What Can You Do?

Seeing the bigger picture

With all the moving parts, and scope of the issue, of globally available clean water it can be easy to assume the task is insurmountable. This is not the case. The key is to not view the goal as a mountain to climb, but a series of many small mountains that each must be reached to move forward.

The power of digital engagement

Especially within this growing digital age, it is much easier to communicate and organize across a larger area than was possible 10–20 years ago. A 2023 study found that within the area of campaigning, digital uptake was up to four times as effective as traditional one-way messaging such as flyers or door to door (Altman et al., 2023). While this study focused only on environmental stability and awareness as a general topic, water stability would fall under that label so the results can be assumed to be accurate, indicating a positive boost in awareness when spreading messaging digitally.

Starting where you are

This can be most easily achieved by staying local to your area, as local groups will focus on specific issues more relevant to you. People are much more likely to care about an issue that personally affects them in some way. The most important thing is to conduct your own research and find a group or organization that best aligns with you and your values for what you want to achieve.

Some ways to start looking for these is to go to your local authority’s website — they may have some groups run with government funding, or you could also go in person. Another is to simply search online for environmental groups in your area; most organizations have a digital presence now, so something will likely come up. If nothing does come up, then you could always start your own group: talk to people you know, neighbours, friends, and see if they share your concerns and may want to do something about it.

Finding community

It is impossible to ignore the scale of the issue no matter how it is presented. It will be difficult, but not impossible, and that is the most important thing to remember. Stick together and find your community, wherever you might be.
